# Meeting Transcript: Pre-Launch Review

**Client:** Ridgeline Financial Services
**Date:** November 17, 2025
**Time:** 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM ET
**Attendees:**
- Karen Walsh (Ridgeline, VP of Operations)
- Derek Simmons (Ridgeline, Director of IT)
- Nina Patel (Ridgeline, Head of Compliance)
- Tom Westbrook (Meridian, Engagement Lead)
- Priya Sharma (Meridian, Technical Lead)
- Aisha Patel (Meridian, Business Analyst)
- Marcus Hayes (Ridgeline, Underwriting Team Lead) *first time attending*

---

**Tom:** Welcome everyone, and especially Marcus — glad to have you join us. We're three weeks from the board meeting and I want to make sure we're buttoned up. Let me hand it to Priya for the development update, and then we'll spend time on the launch plan.

**Priya:** Thanks. So Phase 2 — AI-assisted underwriting — has been in beta with the underwriting team for two weeks now. Marcus, do you want to share how it's going from your perspective?

**Marcus:** Sure. Honestly? I was ready to hate it. Twenty years in underwriting, I was not thrilled about a computer telling me how to do my job. But after two weeks... it's growing on me. The data summary alone saves me 15-20 minutes per application. I used to flip between three screens to get the full picture. Now it's all on one screen with the AI's analysis right there.

**Karen:** And the recommendations? Are they accurate?

**Marcus:** Mostly. For straightforward applications — good credit, stable income, reasonable amounts — it's spot on. I'd say I agree with the AI about 85% of the time on those. Where it struggles is edge cases. Had one applicant with a 680 score but he'd just paid off $40K in student loans six months ago. The AI flagged the recent high debt and recommended a higher interest rate, but any experienced underwriter would see the trajectory and know this guy's credit is heading to 750.

**Priya:** That's a perfect example of where the human judgment adds value. The AI sees the snapshot, the underwriter sees the story.

**Marcus:** Exactly. And I'll say this — my team is faster. Our average processing time is down to 7 days during the beta period, from 12. And that's with the team still getting used to the new workflow.

**Karen:** Seven days. Down from twelve. That's incredible for a beta period.

**Tom:** And we expect that to improve further as the team gets comfortable. The target was 3-5 days and I think we'll get there within a month of full launch.

**Nina:** What about the override rate? I asked for weekly reports.

**Priya:** Yes, I have the data. Overall override rate is 18% — meaning the underwriters disagree with the AI recommendation 18% of the time. Breaking that down: for auto loans under $25K, the override rate is only 8%. For personal loans, it's 22%. And for anything above $50K, it's 35%.

**Nina:** That 35% concerns me. If the AI is wrong a third of the time on large loans, should we even show the recommendation for those?

**Marcus:** I actually think we should. Even when I disagree with the recommendation, the data summary is still valuable. And sometimes the AI makes me think twice — it catches things I might have missed in the numbers. I just don't follow its recommendation as often on the complex ones.

**Tom:** Maybe the right approach is to label the recommendation differently for large loans. Instead of "AI recommends approval," it could say "AI analysis — for reference." Less prescriptive.

**Marcus:** That would be perfect. Takes the pressure off my team to justify every override on big loans.

**Priya:** Easy change. I'll update the UI this week.

**Tom:** Now let's talk launch. Phase 2 full launch is November 21 — this Friday. Are we ready?

**Priya:** From a technical standpoint, yes. All systems are stable. We've processed 340 applications through the beta without any system failures. The audit log is capturing everything correctly. One thing I want to flag — we need to decide on the rollout strategy. Do we go all at once, or phase it by loan type?

**Karen:** What do you recommend?

**Tom:** I'd recommend phased. Start with auto loans under $25K — that's your highest volume and the AI performs best there. Then add personal loans the following week. Larger loans by early December.

**Karen:** Makes sense. Less risk.

**Derek:** I need to raise a production concern. We haven't done a formal load test. During the beta, we had maybe 20 applications per day going through the AI system. Normal volume is 80-100 per day. We need to make sure the system doesn't buckle under full load.

**Priya:** Valid concern. I'll run a load test tomorrow — simulate 200 applications in a single day, which is double the peak volume. If it holds, we're good. If not, we optimize before Friday.

**Derek:** Thank you. I just don't want to be the person explaining to Karen why the system crashed on day one.

**Karen:** Smart thinking, Derek. Let's not have that conversation.

**Tom:** Moving to the customer portal — Phase 3. Priya, where are we?

**Priya:** The portal is 80% complete. Status tracking is working — customers can log in and see which stage their application is in. Notifications are working — email and SMS when the status changes. The 4-hour delay on denials is implemented per Karen's requirement. We're still building the dashboard for Karen's operations team — that's the remaining 20%.

**Karen:** Will it be ready by December 5?

**Priya:** Yes. Barring any surprises in the Phase 2 launch, we'll have the portal ready for December 1 and do a week of internal testing before the public launch.

**Aisha:** I've drafted the board presentation deck for Karen. It covers the problem, the solution, early results from the beta, and the go-forward roadmap. Karen, can we review it together this week?

**Karen:** Wednesday afternoon works. Send me a draft before then so I can review.

**Nina:** I have one final compliance item. I need a sign-off document from Meridian that states the system meets all the compliance requirements we documented. Our legal team needs it for the OCC's annual review in January.

**Tom:** Aisha, can you prepare a compliance attestation document? List every requirement from the spec, the test results, and a formal statement that the system meets them.

**Aisha:** I'll have a draft for Nina by November 28.

**Nina:** That works. And Tom — I want to say, this is the smoothest technology project I've been involved with from a compliance perspective. Usually I'm chasing engineers for documentation. You've been proactive. Thank you.

**Tom:** That's the standard we hold ourselves to. Thank you for being so clear with your requirements — it made our job much easier.

**Karen:** Before we wrap up, one more thing. I've been thinking about what comes after this project. Assuming the December board meeting goes well — and I think it will — I want to discuss expanding the AI capabilities. Specifically, I'm thinking about fraud detection and automated document verification. Is that something Meridian could help with in Q1?

**Tom:** Absolutely. We actually have a case study from a similar engagement — BrightPath Insurance — where we built AI-powered document verification and fraud detection. Let me put together a brief proposal after the launch.

**Karen:** Perfect. Let's have that conversation in December.

**Tom:** Action items: Priya runs load test tomorrow. Phase 2 launches Friday with auto loans first. Priya updates the AI recommendation label for large loans. Aisha reviews the board deck with Karen on Wednesday. Aisha prepares the compliance attestation by November 28. Tom prepares a Q1 expansion brief for Karen. Next meeting December 1 — post-launch review and final Phase 3 preparations.

**Karen:** One last thing — can we schedule a celebration for the team after the board meeting? Assuming it goes well. I want everyone who worked on this to feel appreciated.

**Tom:** I'll plan something. December 15?

**Karen:** Let's do it.
