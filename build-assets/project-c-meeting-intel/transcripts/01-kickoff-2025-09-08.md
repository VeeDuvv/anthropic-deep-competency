# Meeting Transcript: Project Kickoff

**Client:** Ridgeline Financial Services
**Date:** September 8, 2025
**Time:** 10:00 AM - 11:15 AM ET
**Attendees:**
- Karen Walsh (Ridgeline, VP of Operations)
- Derek Simmons (Ridgeline, Director of IT)
- Nina Patel (Ridgeline, Head of Compliance)
- Tom Westbrook (Meridian, Engagement Lead)
- Priya Sharma (Meridian, Technical Lead)
- Aisha Patel (Meridian, Business Analyst)

---

**Tom:** Good morning everyone. Thanks for making time for this kickoff. Before we dive in, I want to make sure we're all aligned on what we're here to accomplish over the next four months. Karen, do you want to set the stage from Ridgeline's perspective?

**Karen:** Sure. So the short version is, our loan processing is too slow. We're averaging 12 business days from application to decision, and our competitors are doing it in 3-5. We're losing customers — our application abandonment rate hit 34% last quarter, which is, frankly, embarrassing. The board has made this a top priority for Q4.

**Derek:** I should add some context on the technical side. We have three separate systems involved in the loan process — the application portal, the underwriting engine, and our core banking system. They don't talk to each other well. A lot of the delay is manual data entry between systems.

**Priya:** That's really helpful. When you say they don't talk to each other, are we talking about completely separate databases, or is there some integration layer?

**Derek:** Completely separate. The application portal is a .NET app we built in 2018. The underwriting engine is a vendor product — LoanPro — and then our core banking is FIS. There's a team of six people whose entire job is basically copying data from one system to another.

**Nina:** And from compliance, I need to flag that whatever we build has to maintain our audit trail. We had an OCC examination last spring and they were already concerned about our documentation. We can't make the process faster at the expense of compliance.

**Tom:** Absolutely. Compliance is a first-class requirement, not an afterthought. Aisha, can you make sure that's front and center in the requirements doc?

**Aisha:** Already noting it. Nina, can we schedule a separate session this week to go through the specific compliance requirements? I want to make sure I capture the OCC feedback accurately.

**Nina:** Thursday works for me. Morning if possible.

**Aisha:** I'll send an invite for Thursday at 9 AM.

**Tom:** Great. So let me outline what we see as the three main workstreams. First, integration — connecting your three systems so data flows automatically. Second, automation — using AI to accelerate document review and underwriting decisions on straightforward applications. Third, the customer experience — giving applicants real-time status updates instead of the current black hole.

**Karen:** That third one is huge. Right now when someone applies, they hear nothing for 12 days and then get an email. We've done surveys — the uncertainty is what drives abandonment. People apply with us and then go to a competitor because at least the competitor tells them where they are in the process.

**Derek:** On the integration piece, I have a concern. LoanPro — our underwriting vendor — their API is... not great. We tried to integrate with it two years ago and gave up. Their documentation is outdated and their support team takes weeks to respond.

**Priya:** We've actually worked with LoanPro on another engagement. Their v3 API is much better than v2. Let me check which version you're on and we can assess. If we need to, we can build an adapter layer. But let's not assume it's a blocker until we've looked at it.

**Derek:** That would be great. We're definitely on v2 right now.

**Tom:** Okay, so action items from today. Aisha, you'll schedule the compliance deep-dive with Nina for Thursday. Priya, you'll audit the current systems and the LoanPro API situation — can we have a technical assessment by end of next week?

**Priya:** End of next week is tight but doable. I'll need access to all three environments.

**Derek:** I'll get you credentials by tomorrow. I'll also send over the architecture diagrams we have, although fair warning, they might be outdated.

**Tom:** Karen, for the customer experience workstream, can you share the survey data on abandonment? And any competitive benchmarks you have on processing times?

**Karen:** I'll have my team pull that together. Give me until Wednesday.

**Tom:** Perfect. One more thing — timeline. Karen, you mentioned the board wants this for Q4. Are we talking end of Q4, or is there a specific date?

**Karen:** The board meeting is December 12. I need to present results, not plans. So ideally we'd have the first phase live by early December, even if it's not the full solution.

**Tom:** That's aggressive but achievable if we stay focused. Let's target November 28 for Phase 1 go-live, giving us two weeks of buffer before the board meeting.

**Karen:** I like that.

**Nina:** Just to circle back — I want to make sure the AI component has human oversight. We can't have a machine auto-approving loans without a human review, at least not initially. The regulators would have concerns.

**Tom:** Completely agree. Our approach would be AI-assisted, not AI-autonomous. The system recommends, a human decides. Over time, as we build confidence and the regulators get comfortable, we can expand automation. But day one, a human signs off on every decision.

**Nina:** That works for me.

**Tom:** Alright, I think we have a clear picture. Next meeting in two weeks — same time, same group. We'll present the technical assessment and a detailed project plan. Thanks everyone.

**Karen:** Thank you. I'm feeling much better about this than I was an hour ago.
