# Proposal: Ironforge Industries — ERP Modernization

**Client:** Ironforge Industries (metal fabrication, 3 plants, $600M revenue)
**Date:** April 2025
**Value:** $2.1M over 16 months
**Status:** Pending
**Team:** Tom Westbrook (lead), Marcus Chen, Priya Sharma, + 6 additional engineers
**Practice:** Digital Strategy + Application Development

## Executive Summary

Ironforge's 15-year-old on-premise ERP (SAP ECC) is end-of-support in 2027. The system is heavily customized with 300+ custom ABAP programs. Rather than a costly SAP S/4HANA migration ($8-12M estimated), we propose a best-of-breed cloud-native approach: core ERP on a modern platform (NetSuite or Dynamics 365), with custom modules rebuilt as microservices where they provide competitive advantage, and standard modules replaced with SaaS products.

## Approach

**Phase 1 (Months 1-4):** Deep analysis of 300 custom programs — categorize as replace (SaaS exists), rebuild (competitive advantage), or retire (no longer needed). Tom Westbrook leads the business case for each.

**Phase 2 (Months 5-10):** Core ERP implementation (NetSuite preferred). Data migration from SAP. Rebuild 40-50 custom modules as cloud-native microservices.

**Phase 3 (Months 11-16):** Integration, testing, parallel run, cutover. Change management and training for 800 users across 3 plants. Decommission SAP.

## Key Differentiators

- Tom Westbrook has led 4 ERP modernization assessments and knows which custom code to keep vs. kill
- Best-of-breed approach saves $6-10M compared to S/4HANA migration
- Marcus Chen's cloud architecture ensures new platform scales across all 3 plants
- Change management built in — Aisha Patel-style stakeholder engagement
